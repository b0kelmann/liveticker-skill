LiveTicker

A real-time coordination layer for live events.
100 stakeholders become 100 sensors — one agent weaves them.

Live work — GOSIM Hackathon 2026 OpenClaw track Solo (b0kelmann) Apache-2.0 Target: Z.AI Innovation Award · $2k

1The problem & the move

Every live event has the same broken loop: a few organizers can't see everything, and the people who can see something happening have no easy way to surface it. Bottlenecks form. Tragedies (Astroworld 2021) happen.

The pain

1 staff member per ~100 attendees. Organizers can't be everywhere, talks run long, lunch lines back up, crowds form unsafe clusters at one stage while another sits empty. By the time someone notices, it's too late.

Information about reality lives in dozens of heads, none of them connected.

LiveTicker's move

Turn every stakeholder into a sensor. Fans, crew, security, medics — they each have a simple channel where they can ask, signal, or post. An LLM-backed agent fuses all signals against an explicit Plan, Risk Catalog, and Goals — and routes the right action to the right person.

No more bottleneck on coordination — the system handles fanout in seconds.

Think of it as the Bahn delay-display, but for everything happening at a festival — and instead of waiting for one dispatcher, the system itself reads what 30+ people are reporting and routes interventions before things break.

2Architecture

Three explicit knowledge stores, one reasoning engine, three input channels, multi-channel output. The agent is structured — not a black box.

Plan State
What should happen
Schedule, resources, roles, capacity targets — the event's intended trajectory.
Reality State
What's actually happening
Live signals from stakeholders — their messages, questions, status reports — with extracted sensor info.
Risk Catalog
What could go wrong
Multi-author library of patterns + suggested actions. Editable live by stakeholders during the event.
↓ all three flow into ↓
Reasoning Engine (LLM)
Plan ↔ Reality ↔ Risk diff, in context of Event Goals + Stakeholder Taxonomy
"Where does Reality drift from Plan? Does it match a Risk pattern? Whose Goals are advanced/threatened? Who needs to know, in what tone, via what channel?"
↓
Output Router
Per-stakeholder fanout
One signal becomes N tailored notifications — Security, Medics, Artists, Fans, PA — each in the right register.

Three input channels (separated, OpenClaw skill capabilities)

ticker.post — share an update
all
ticker.signal — flag drift / risk
stakeholders
ticker.ask — question (also: implicit sensor)
all

When 5 fans ask "where's stage A?" within 60 seconds, that's not just 5 questions — it's a signal of signage failure that the agent automatically surfaces to the organizer.

4Architectural decisions (settled)

14 design questions resolved through a Socratic grilling session. Each decision has a rationale; future Claude sessions should respect them unless explicitly revisited.

Q1
Aha-moment: dynamic event coordination — not "smart router", not "auto-broadcaster"
Q2
Tool type: coordination-tool (not schedule-tool)
Q3
Agent form: Voll Input-Fusion — agent senses on its own, not just routes external inputs
Q4
Architecture spine: Plan State / Reality State / Risk Catalog as three explicit stores
Q5
Stakeholder Graph: first-class citizen — built into the data model from Day 1
Q6
Input channels: 3 separated (ticker.post, ticker.signal, ticker.ask)
Q7-bis
Demo format: Audience-as-System — QR scan + roles, audience drives the demo
Q8
Goal granularity: Stufe B — Goals + Drivers in YAML, organizer can edit live in demo
Q11
Event scenario: Festival, Tomorrowland-style, Day 2, 19:30 before headliner
Q12
Time frame: Frozen Moment with countdown — demo time = story time, max drama
Q13
Scale framing: Big-Frame — claim festival-real numbers, demo a small subset
Q14
Killer-Moment: Crowd-Crush Detection (Astroworld pattern) — multi-channel fanout

Open decisions

Q16?
Frontend tech stack — Plain HTML+JS vs. Telegram Bot vs. React. Default: Plain HTML.
Q17?
Hosting for live demo — ngrok vs. Vercel/Railway. Default: ngrok with cloud fallback.
Q18?
Backstage-plant inputs as safety net for killer-moment. Default: yes, ~3 plants.
